Sushi Recipe (Tuna)

Ingredients

Sushi Rice

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Tuna Sushi

  • 1 lb fresh sushi-grade tuna (e.g., bluefin or yellowfin)
  • Soy sauce (for serving)
  • Wasabi (for serving)
  • Pickled ginger (for serving)

For Maki Rolls

  • 4 sheets of nori (seaweed)
  • 1 cucumber (julienned)
  • 1 avocado (slided)
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Preparation Steps

  1. Prepare Sushi Rice
    2.
    1. Rinse Rice: Rinse 2 cups of sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    2. Cook Rice: In a rice cooker or pot, combine the rinsed rice and 2 cups of water. Cook according to rice cooker instructions or bring to a boil, then simmer for 20 minutes.
    3. Season Rice: In a small saucepan, heat 1/4 cup rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons sugar, and 1 teaspoon salt until dissolved. Mix with the cooked rice and let it cool.

  2. Prepare Tuna
    3.
    1. Slice Tuna: Using a sharp knife, slice the fresh tuna into thin pieces for nigiri (about 1/4 inch thick) and into strips for maki (about 1/2 inch wide).

  3. Make Tuna Nigiri
    4.
    1. Form Rice Mounds: Wet your hands and take a small amount of sushi rice (about 1 tablespoon). Form it into an oval mound.
    2. Top with Tuna: Place a slice of tuna on top of each rice mound.
    3. Serve: Arrange the nigiri on a plate and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

  4. Make Tuna Maki Rolls
    1. Prepare Rolling Mat: Place a bamboo sushi rolling mat on a flat surface and cover it with plastic wrap.
    2. Lay Nori: Place one sheet of nori on the mat, shiny side down.
    3. Spread Rice: Wet your hands and spread an even layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving a small border at the top.
    4. Add Fillings: In the center of the rice, add a few strips of tuna, cucumber, and avocado.
    5. Roll Sushi: Starting from the bottom, use the mat to roll the sushi away from you, applying gentle pressure. Seal the edge of the nori with a little water.
    6. Slice Rolls: Using a sharp knife, slice the roll into 6-8 pieces.
    7. Serve: Arrange the slices on a plate and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

Enjoy Your Tuna Sushi

This recipe provides a straightforward way to prepare delicious tuna sushi, whether as nigiri or maki rolls.
